What it costs to start a handyman painting business

Short answer: about $1,400 — and the paint gear is the cheap part.

The tools: about $360

Scraper set$50
Sanding blocks$20
Roller kit + disposable liners$40
Brush set incl. cutting-in brush$30
Extension pole$20
Short ladder$100
Drop sheets$60
Rags, broom & dustpan, garbage bags$35
Total tools~$360

Starter jobs are repaints, and repaints are mostly preparation — so prep gear leads the list. Deliberately missing: sprayer, branded workwear, sign-written vehicle, expensive website. The biggest startup mistake is buying the gear of the business you hope to have instead of what the jobs you can win now require.

The consumables rule: tape, filler, cleaner and paint aren't startup costs — they're job costs, and they belong on the client's quote every time. Blur that line and you're paying for the client's materials out of your own labour.

The paperwork: the expensive part

Liability insurance — budget around $1,000/year. It's the difference between a spilled tin on an expensive floor being a bad afternoon or a serious financial loss. Working in other people's homes uninsured isn't a saving; it's a bet.

Registration and licensing — varies by location. Some places allow small unlicensed jobs under a dollar threshold; others require licensing from the first dollar. There is no universal rule, and checking yours before your first quote is your responsibility. The video description below includes an AI research prompt that will find your official local regulators for you.

What costs nothing

The vehicle — the car you already own carries a $360 kit. And marketing — first jobs come from people who know you, local referrals, and property managers who always need small repaint work. Paid ads earn their place after your pricing works.

The gear gets you to the door. The quote decides if you make money walking through it.
